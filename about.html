ABOUT FOOTNOTE

Every headline has a footnote.

Footnote started with a simple frustration: headlines tell us what happened, but rarely give us enough space to ask why.

What came before the story? What gets left out? Whose perspective are we missing? And what happens after everyone stops talking about it?

Footnote is an independent space built around those questions. It explores stories through history, law, economics, and perspectives that don't always fit inside a headline.

Why I built it

I built Footnote because I wanted somewhere curiosity could go beyond the news cycle. Not another place to repeat headlines, but a place to examine what sits underneath them.

Footnote doesn't try to give you one final answer. It gives you more ways to ask the question.
