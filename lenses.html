ONE HEADLINE · THREE LENSES

A headline changes depending on where you stand.

News rarely belongs to one subject. Explore the same issue through history, law, and economics — and watch the story change.

TODAY'S QUESTION

What happens after a protest leaves the headlines?

LENS 01

History asks: what came before?

A protest rarely begins on the day people enter the streets. Historical decisions, earlier movements, political promises and unresolved conflicts can shape demands for years before they become a headline.

LENS 02

Law asks: what can actually change?

Public pressure can demand reform, but lasting change often moves through constitutions, courts, legislation and institutions. Looking through a legal lens separates a political demand from the mechanisms required to make it enforceable.

LENS 03

Economics asks: who bears the cost?

Political decisions also distribute resources, opportunities and costs. An economic lens asks who gains, who loses, and whether the material conditions behind a movement remain after public attention disappears.
