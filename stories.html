INDIA / POLITICS & SOCIETY

Jantar Mantar, Uncensored

Jantar Mantar has become more than a landmark in the centre of New Delhi. For decades, it has been a meeting point between citizens and power — a place where farmers, students, activists, athletes, workers, and ordinary people have gathered when they believed their voices were not being heard elsewhere.

But a protest rarely fits inside a headline. Behind the slogans are questions about who gets heard, how movements survive after public attention disappears, and whether demonstrations actually change the institutions they challenge.

Footnote looks beyond the spectacle of protest to examine what Jantar Mantar reveals about dissent, democracy, public space, and the complicated relationship between India's citizens and the state.
